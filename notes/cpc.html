1. Introduction

What is mathematical logic? As the name says, it is a subject that studies the logic by mathematical approach. But, what make it is possible to use logic to study itself? I think, the logos is a innate property of our human beings, and we are just using the formal method to reflect some aspects of it. Different formalization can allow us to have a glimpse of different aspect of the human reason.

In this process, we are building a hierarchy of meta languages and object languages, the former is the language(and the theory) we used to think and to study the latter. In this case, the meta language is the mathematical language, which is English with some mathematical symbols; and the object language is the formal system of the logic we investigated.

In this series of articles, we introduce classical propositional logic as a starting point, which represents a kind of composed propositions from some basic facts by connectives and the reasoning in these kind of propositions.

2. Proof Theory

Now we develop the proof theory of the classical propositional logic(or propositional calculus, in the sense of syntactical deduction). We introduce three systems: Hilbert style axiom system (LHK), natural deduction(NK) and Gentzen style sequent calculus(LK). Then we shall prove these systems are equivalent, and then investigate the concept of provability and consistency.

2.1. LHK

The deduction systems are always have axioms and rules, the Hilbert system is a canonical one – it has three axioms and one rule. It is very popular in logic text because prove some property of the whole calculus is very easy if choose it to describe the concept of provability.
